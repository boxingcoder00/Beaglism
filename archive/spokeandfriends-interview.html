Interview with Yuyita

Life with Three Beagles: An Owner's Tale

Published on September 3nd , 2025 ‚Ä¢ [10] min read
The story of the three musketeers.

Yuyita and her 3 little beagle dogs journey: From their origins to where they stand today

The wolfpack living their best life!!
Ever asked what its like to live with 3 beagles?? We sat down with Yuyita to hear about her experience with 3 furry beagles and what life is really like with these lovable hounds. Lets begin!!
Q:
How many beagles do you have and what are their names? Have you had beagles before?
A:
I have three beagles: Spock, 12, born June 19, 2013, Kirk, 10, born June 13, 2015, and Ahsoka, 4, born February 2, 2021. This is my first time owning beagles
Q:
How did this beagle come into your life? Was it planned or love at first sight?
A:
The first to arrive is Spock. I had just lost my cocker spaniel (Rocky), who had died at age 16 from kidney failure. I was very sick and devastated since we were very close. My husband was also ill, and a coworker of his advised us to look for another furry friend since that was the best way to deal with the grief and would help us recover from such a strong blow. My husband asked me what breed I liked and I told him beagle, and that's when the search began. Spock arrived at 40 days old. I was so fascinated by this breed that at age 2 I decided to look for another one and give Spock a companion to play with, and that's how Kirk arrived. After 6 years, my oldest son wanted one and Ahsoka arrived.
Spock the first takes the spotlight
"I adopted him, we adopted him, and I renamed him because I thought that with a new name, I could leave his heartbreak behind and start his new life. And so Iker was born, at the end of November 2020. Leaving abuse behind and ready to learn what it means to be loved."
Kirk now joins the spotlight!
Q:
What has been the funniest or most emotional moment you have experienced with your beagle?
A:
The funniest moments have been when he steals food, socks, or toilet paper and runs away to hide.
Q:
If your beagle could talk for a day, what do you think it would say to you?
A:
He would tell me how grateful he is for the family he has and how much he loves us.
Q:
What is the most memorable prank your beagle has ever done?
A:
Chasing iguanas which I don't like that they do at all!
Q:
What surprised you most about your beagle's personality when you brought him home?
A:
Their energy to play and run are very energetic furry and their sense of smell to track prey.
Q:
What activities do you enjoy most together?
A:
Since we live on an island, we often all go to the beach to enjoy the sea breeze and we also walk a lot in the area where we live.
Q:
What advice would you give to someone considering adopting a beagle for the first time?
A:
They are excellent pets to raise with children, because they are very sweet, affectionate, and not at all aggressive dogs.
Q:
Is there anything else you'd like readers to know about owning beagles?
A:
Beagles are very energetic, hungry dogs with a keen sense of smell, but they are also very sweet and affectionate. They are very affectionate with humans and do not like to be left alone at all. However, I also consider them to be very special beings and excellent companions for children with disabilities. Beagles, despite their energy and tireless sense of smell, are excellent pets to keep at home and also like being with people. If they are well trained and educated, they can become excellent pets. For me, they are one of the best breeds, as long as their owner is a responsible person who knows and meets all their needs. For me, they are magical beings and unique beings, and if I had to choose again, I would choose a Beagle again.
Life is better with a beagle - Iker living his best life in his new chapter.
