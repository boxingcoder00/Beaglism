Overview

Between 100,000 to 200,000 animals are tested annually for cosmetics and household products in the United States alone.

While some companies have committed to cruelty-free practices, many major corporations still conduct or pay for animal testing. Rabbits, guinea pigs, mice, and rats suffer in laboratories to test beauty products and cleaning supplies.

Good news: Modern alternatives exist that are faster, cheaper, and more accurate than animal testing.

Types of Testing Conducted

Draize Test

Substances are applied to restrained rabbits' eyes. The test lasts 7-21 days and often causes blindness and severe injury.

Skin Irritation Testing

Test substances are rubbed on shaved skin of guinea pigs or rabbits for up to 14 days. Can cause severe burns and scarring.

Oral Toxicity Testing (LD50)

Animals are force-fed substances to determine lethal doses. Results in severe suffering and death.

Repeat Dose Toxicity

Animals inhale, ingest, or absorb substances for 90 days or longer. Results in organ damage, cancer, and death.

Modern Alternatives to Animal Testing

Advanced Methods Available Now:

  • In Vitro Testing: Uses human skin cells in a lab - faster, cheaper, more accurate
  • 3D Bioprinting: Creates human-like tissue structures for testing
  • Organ-on-a-Chip: Microfluidic devices that simulate organ function
  • AI & Machine Learning: Analyzes existing data to predict toxicity without new tests
  • Computational Models: Computer simulations test thousands of compounds instantly
Why alternatives are better:
  • 90% of drugs that pass animal tests fail in human trials
  • Results available in weeks instead of months/years
  • Much less expensive
  • More accurate at predicting human responses

How to Help & Make a Difference

Buy Cruelty-Free Products

  • Look for certifications: Leaping Bunny (gold standard), PETA Certified, Cruelty Free International
  • Use apps: Cruelty Cutter or Leaping Bunny app to check products
  • Check parent companies: Sometimes a brand is cruelty-free but its parent company isn't
  • Support independent brands: Pacifica, Derma E, Acure, Burt's Bees, Nyx (select products)

Contact Companies

  • Email major corporations asking them to end animal testing
  • Sign petitions on PETA and Cruelty Free International websites
  • Share your concerns on their social media

Support Organizations Fighting Animal Testing

  • PETA - peta.org
  • Beagle Freedom Project - beaglefreedomproject.org
  • Cruelty Free International - crueltyfreeinternational.org
  • Humane Society - humanesociety.org

Advocate for Change

  • Contact your state representative about banning cosmetics animal testing
  • Support legislation like the Beagle Bill
  • Share information with friends and family

Take Action This Week

  • Replace one personal care product with a cruelty-free alternative
  • Download the Cruelty Cutter app and check your products
  • Sign a petition against animal testing
  • Share this article on social media

Good News: Regulations Are Changing

EU Ban (2009): Europe completely banned cosmetics animal testing.

California (2020): Banned animal testing for cosmetics ingredients.

FDA Modernization Acts (2022, 2024): US is requiring alternatives to animal testing.

Illinois and other states: Implementing similar bans.

This shows that cosmetics CAN be developed without animal testing. Many companies still use it because the regulations allow it, not because it's necessary.
