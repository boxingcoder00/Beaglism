Find Your Perfect Beagle Companion in Florida

Beagles are one of America's most beloved dog breeds — cheerful, affectionate, and endlessly curious. Their gentle temperament makes them wonderful companions for families, seniors, and singles alike. But each year, thousands of beagles end up in shelters or rescue situations across Florida due to abandonment, neglect, or owners who weren't prepared for the breed's energetic, hound-driven personality.

That's where Florida's dedicated beagle rescue organizations come in. These volunteer-run nonprofits work tirelessly to pull beagles from shelters, provide medical care and rehabilitation, place them in loving foster homes, and match them with the right forever families. Adopting through a rescue is often more affordable than buying from a breeder, and you'll be giving a dog a second chance at a happy life.

Below, we've compiled the best beagle rescues in Florida — with details on each organization's mission, coverage area, and how to start the adoption process.

Tampa Bay Beagle Rescue

📍 Tampa, FL — Serves all of Florida

Tampa Bay Beagle Rescue (TBBR) is widely regarded as one of the premier beagle rescue organizations in the state. As an all-volunteer, foster-based 501(c)(3) nonprofit, TBBR specializes in rescuing purebred beagles and beagle mixes from shelters and abuse situations across Florida.

Rather than operating a physical shelter, rescued dogs live in the homes of dedicated foster families — giving each dog a calm, nurturing environment to decompress and heal before moving to their forever home. TBBR provides full veterinary care, including treatment for sick, injured, and elderly beagles, and all dogs are spayed or neutered prior to adoption.

The organization has rehomed over 200 beagles in a single year, making it one of the highest-volume beagle rescues in Florida. Their adoption counselors carefully match each dog to the right family, paying special attention to placing beagles with special needs — older dogs or those with ongoing medical conditions — into ideal homes.

SouthEast Beagle Rescue (SEBR)

📍 Dunnellon, FL — FL, GA, AL, LA, TN & SC

Founded in 2011, SouthEast Beagle Rescue (SEBR) is a well-established, all-volunteer 501(c)(3) organization based in Dunnellon, Florida. Their mission is to rescue, rehabilitate, and rehome unwanted, abandoned, and abused beagles and beagle mixes — and their reach extends well beyond Florida into several southeastern states.

SEBR's team of experienced volunteers is well-trained in animal rescue and transport, enabling them to pull beagles from shelters across a wide geographic area. All dogs are spayed or neutered before adoption, and the organization serves as an educational resource for beagle owners in the community.

One of SEBR's standout features is their unique "sleepover" adoption model: once your adoption survey is approved, you can take your potential new companion home for a trial period before finalizing the adoption — ensuring a great match for both dog and family. They also host regular weekend adoption events where you can meet available beagles in person.

South Florida Beagle Rescue

📍 Miami, FL — South Florida Region

South Florida Beagle Rescue is a nonprofit animal rescue and advocacy organization based in Miami, committed to connecting the humans and hounds of South Florida. Since their founding, they have found loving homes for more than 140 beagles and hound-type dogs — a remarkable achievement for a foster-based organization that relies entirely on volunteers, fosters, adopters, and donors.

Their mission goes beyond adoption: South Florida Beagle Rescue is a vocal advocate for a cruelty-free lifestyle and works to ease overcrowding in local county shelters through active partnerships. They specialize in rescuing homeless, stray, neglected, and abandoned beagles and beagle mixes throughout the South Florida area.

Like many quality rescues, their process involves a pre-approval step before you can take a dog home. After approval, they use a unique "slumber party" model — you take the beagle home for a few days to see if it's the right fit before finalizing the adoption.

Tips for Adopting a Beagle in Florida

🐕 Know the Breed First

Beagles are scent hounds bred to follow their noses. They can be stubborn, vocal, and escape-prone. A securely fenced yard is highly recommended. Research the breed thoroughly before applying — every reputable Florida rescue will encourage this.

📋 Prepare Your Application

Most Florida beagle rescues require an online application, veterinary references, and a phone interview. Be honest about your lifestyle, home setup, and experience with dogs. Approvals typically take 7–14 days.

🏡 Fenced Yard Preferred

Beagles are natural wanderers with a powerful nose that can override their recall. A securely fenced yard isn't always required, but it's strongly preferred by most Florida rescue organizations. Electric fences are generally not accepted.

🤝 Consider Fostering First

All three major Florida beagle rescues are foster-based and always need more foster families. Fostering is a great way to experience life with a beagle before committing to adoption — and you'll be directly saving a life.

🩺 All Dogs Come Vetted

Reputable Florida beagle rescues spay or neuter all dogs before adoption, provide vaccinations, microchipping, and treat any known medical conditions. Ask each rescue for a full health history before finalizing your adoption.

💰 Adoption Fees Save Money

Adoption fees from Florida rescues typically range from $100–$400 — far less than breeder prices, and the dog arrives already vetted. Fees help cover the rescue's medical and care costs for all the dogs they serve.

Frequently Asked Questions About Beagle Rescues in Florida

Are beagles good dogs for first-time owners in Florida?

Beagles can be a challenge for first-time dog owners due to their stubborn, independent nature and tendency to follow their nose. That said, they're affectionate, social, and great with children and other pets. Florida's rescue organizations often provide guidance and breed education to help new owners succeed. If you're a novice, ask your rescue for an easygoing adult beagle rather than a puppy.

How long does it take to adopt a beagle from a Florida rescue?

The adoption process typically takes between 1–3 weeks from initial application to bringing your dog home. Tampa Bay Beagle Rescue notes that application approval alone takes about 7–10 days. You'll then need time to find the right match and, with some organizations like SEBR and South Florida Beagle Rescue, complete a trial period or "sleepover" before finalizing.

Do I need a fenced yard to adopt a beagle in Florida?

Most Florida beagle rescues strongly recommend a securely fenced yard, as beagles have powerful instincts to follow scents and can easily escape. Some rescues require it outright. Electronic or invisible fences are typically not accepted as a substitute. Always check the specific requirements of the rescue you're applying to.

Can I adopt a beagle in Florida if I live in another state?

Some Florida rescues, like SouthEast Beagle Rescue, have volunteer networks and transport capabilities that span multiple southeastern states including Georgia, Alabama, Louisiana, Tennessee, and South Carolina. Contact your rescue of choice to find out whether out-of-state adoptions are possible.

What's the difference between a rescue and a shelter?

Florida's beagle-specific rescues are typically foster-based — meaning dogs live in volunteer homes rather than a facility. This allows caregivers to understand each dog's personality and needs in a real home environment, which leads to better adoption matches. Traditional shelters house dogs in kennels and serve a much broader range of animals.

How can I support Florida beagle rescues if I can't adopt right now?

You can volunteer as a foster family, donate directly to the rescue, assist with transport, or help at adoption events. All three major Florida beagle rescues are all-volunteer organizations that rely entirely on community support to fund their operations and save more dogs.
